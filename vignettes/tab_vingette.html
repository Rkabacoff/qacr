Introduction to the tab function

library(qacr)

The tab function provides a frequency table for a categorical variable. Many options are available.

The venues data frame contains information on major sports venues. First, let’s tabulate the status of the venues.

tab(venues, status)
#>                level   n percent
#> 1             CLOSED   6      1%
#> 2               OPEN 813     99%
#> 3 UNDER CONSTRUCTION   5      1%

Next, we’ll tabulate the sites by country, sorting from highest to lowest. We set digits equal to 4 and add a “Total” category.

tab(venues, country, sort=TRUE, total=TRUE, digits=4)
#>   level   n percent
#> 1   usa 785  95.27%
#> 2   mex  21   2.55%
#> 3   can  17   2.06%
#> 4   pri   1   0.12%
#> 5 Total 824    100%

Next, let’s tabulate the sites by state, sorting from largest number to smallest number of sites. We’ll remove all missing observations from the data set, add a total row, and limit the states to the 10 most frequent, plus an “Other” category.

tab(venues, state, sort = TRUE, na.rm = TRUE, total = TRUE, maxcat=10)
#>    level   n percent
#> 1     CA  70      8%
#> 2     TX  59      7%
#> 3     FL  43      5%
#> 4     NY  43      5%
#> 5     NC  34      4%
#> 6     OH  34      4%
#> 7     IL  28      3%
#> 8     PA  27      3%
#> 9     LA  24      3%
#> 10    VA  23      3%
#> 11 Other 439     53%
#> 12 Total 824    100%

Finally, let’s list the states that have at least 5% of the venues, combining the rest into an “Other” category.

tab(venues, state,  minp=0.05)
#>   level   n percent
#> 1    CA  70      8%
#> 2    FL  43      5%
#> 3    NY  43      5%
#> 4    TX  59      7%
#> 5 Other 609     74%
